# Go-To-Market

## 1. GTM Strategy Overview

The go-to-market motion should build trust from developer-first tools, expand through ecosystem participation, and mature into enterprise infrastructure. The platform should be sold as Taiwan futures quant infrastructure, not as a profit guarantee.

The current four-entry growth motion is documented in
`docs/web-app-growth-strategy.md`. It connects the marketing website, Web App
Interactive Demo, Facebook Page, and Facebook Group into one loop:

```text
education content -> website trust -> browser-only Web App demo -> moderated community feedback -> product and content iteration
```

The Web App remains a browser-only Interactive Demo and Paper Trading research
platform where applicable. It must not be positioned as a live trading surface,
broker login flow, customer account system, or production trading product.

## 2. Phase 1: Tool-led Growth

Goal: build credibility and user pipeline.

Tactics:
- Free rollover-aware backtesting tools.
- Free TX/MTX/TMF exposure calculator.
- Free paper-trading sandbox.
- Developer-first content.
- Public architecture notes and safety defaults.

## 3. Phase 2: Ecosystem Expansion

Goal: increase ARPU and retention.

Tactics:
- Broker API readiness and future integration partnerships.
- Strategy marketplace planning.
- AI strategy diagnostics.
- Pro and Elite plans.
- Community strategy review workflow.

## 4. Phase 3: Infrastructure Moat

Goal: long-term ARR and high switching costs.

Tactics:
- Enterprise licensing.
- Private cloud deployment.
- RBAC/ABAC.
- WORM audit logs.
- SLA.
- Security and compliance procurement readiness.

## 5. Initial Lead Magnets

- TX/MTX/TMF exposure calculator.
- Rollover-aware backtest data demo.
- Paper trading sandbox.
- Data quality checklist.
- Strategy risk review template.
- Paper-to-shadow readiness checklist.

## 6. Developer and Trader Content Strategy

Content themes:
- TX-equivalent exposure sizing.
- Rollover handling.
- Data quality.
- Backtest reproducibility.
- Paper trading discipline.
- Strategy/execution separation.
- Risk Engine and OMS boundaries.

Facebook execution assets:
- `docs/facebook-community-launch-plan.md`
- `docs/facebook-content-calendar.md`
- `docs/web-app-growth-strategy.md`

Facebook should be used as the broad education and community channel for Taiwan futures researchers. Keep the channel focused on data governance, backtesting discipline, Paper Only demos, and platform safety boundaries.

The first activation milestone is completion of the browser-only demo and
understanding why StrategySignal, Risk Engine, OMS, Paper Broker simulation, and
evidence remain separate workflow stages.

## 7. Broker Partnership Motion

Broker partnerships should start as infrastructure or integration discussions. Any fee-sharing, referral, signal, or order-routing revenue requires legal and regulatory review.

Partner assets:
- Broker gateway abstraction notes.
- Paper-only integration demos.
- Security and auditability brief.
- Data governance and reconciliation roadmap.

## 8. Enterprise Sales Motion

Enterprise sales should emphasize:
- governance
- auditability
- data versioning
- private deployment
- RBAC/ABAC
- SLA
- security review support
- clear compliance boundary

## 9. Community and Strategy Marketplace Growth

Marketplace growth requires trust:
- author profiles
- strategy validation metadata
- paper/shadow history
- risk labels
- review workflow
- clear revenue split

It must not imply that users can copy strategies for profit without risk or legal review.

## 10. KPI Dashboard

Required KPIs:
- free to Basic conversion
- Basic to Pro conversion
- Pro to Elite conversion
- enterprise pipeline value
- annual recurring revenue
- net revenue retention
- churn
- active strategy runners
- data API usage
- AI diagnosis usage
- strategy marketplace GMV

## 11. 90-Day Plan

| Period | Focus | Output |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Days 1-30 | Trust foundation | Safety docs, exposure calculator, paper sandbox copy |
| Days 31-60 | Developer content | Rollover notes, backtest reproducibility guide, waitlist-free demos |
| Days 61-90 | Commercial pilots | Pricing validation, broker conversations, enterprise discovery |

## 12. 12-Month Plan

- Q1: free tooling, paper workflow, and content credibility.
- Q2: Pro/Elite packaging and AI diagnostics pilot.
- Q3: strategy marketplace design and enterprise security package.
- Q4: enterprise pilots and private deployment planning.

## 13. Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
| --- | --- |
| Overpromising profitability | Use infrastructure-first copy and compliance review |
| Regulatory ambiguity | Label high-risk models compliance-dependent |
| Data licensing issues | Keep data rights explicit |
| Long enterprise cycles | Use paid pilots and implementation services |
| Broker dependency | Maintain broker gateway abstraction |
