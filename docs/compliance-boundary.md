# Compliance Boundary

## 1. Purpose

This document defines safer commercial and marketing boundaries for the Taifex Quant Trading Platform. It is not legal advice. Consult licensed legal counsel before regulated offerings.

## 2. Early-Stage SaaS Boundary

The early product should be positioned as SaaS technical infrastructure:
- research tooling
- paper trading
- infrastructure
- risk controls
- auditability
- user-controlled workflows
- compliance-dependent future service where relevant

## 3. What the Platform Can Say

Safer wording:
- "Research tooling for Taiwan futures quant workflows."
- "Paper trading and shadow trading infrastructure."
- "Risk controls and OMS boundaries."
- "Auditability for teams and enterprise review."
- "User-controlled workflows for strategy research and monitoring."
- "Compliance-dependent future service."
- "Consult licensed legal counsel before regulated offerings."

## 4. What the Platform Must Not Say

Prohibited or high-risk phrases:
- guaranteed profit
- risk-free
- fully automated money machine
- we trade for you
- copy our signals for profit
- guaranteed alpha
- principal guaranteed
- no loss

## 5. Higher-Risk Revenue Models

Higher-risk revenue models require legal review:
- performance fees
- managed accounts
- copy trading
- signal subscriptions
- broker fee-sharing
- discretionary trading
- futures advisory services

## 6. Advisory / Signal / Copy Trading Boundary

The platform must not provide individualized futures trading recommendations by default. Signal subscriptions and copy trading are future compliance-dependent options only.

## 7. Managed Account / Discretionary Trading Boundary

Managed accounts and discretionary trading must not be launched without legal and regulatory review, proper licensing, and operational controls.

## 8. Broker Fee-Sharing Boundary

Broker fee-sharing or referral arrangements can create regulatory and conflict-of-interest issues. Treat them as compliance-dependent future options only.

## 9. Data Licensing Boundary

Data products must respect exchange, vendor, and broker data rights. Customer contracts should define permitted use, redistribution restrictions, retention, and audit rights.

## 10. Enterprise Security and Procurement Boundary

Security and procurement positioning may reference:
- OWASP ASVS alignment as a target
- RBAC/ABAC roadmap
- WORM audit log roadmap
- private deployment direction
- incident response and reconciliation responsibility

Do not claim certification or production readiness unless proven.

## 11. Marketing Copy Checklist

- [ ] No profit guarantee.
- [ ] No risk-free language.
- [ ] No implication that the platform trades for users.
- [ ] High-risk services labeled compliance-dependent.
- [ ] Paper trading defaults visible.
- [ ] User responsibility and futures risk included.
- [ ] No broker credentials or sensitive data collection.

## 12. Contractual Risk Allocation

Enterprise contracts should define:
- kill switch responsibility
- reconciliation responsibility
- audit log retention
- platform availability
- support scope
- data licensing
- incident response
- customer trading decision responsibility

## 13. Review Triggers

Legal review is required before:
- launching signal subscriptions
- enabling copy trading
- accepting external capital
- offering managed accounts
- charging performance fees
- sharing broker fees
- providing individualized futures advice
- collecting sensitive financial information

## 14. Disclaimer Template

This platform is for research, engineering, paper trading, and infrastructure workflows. It is not investment advice, does not guarantee profit, and does not remove the substantial risks of futures trading. Regulated services such as futures advisory, signal subscriptions, copy trading, managed accounts, performance-based fees, or broker fee-sharing require separate legal and regulatory review.
