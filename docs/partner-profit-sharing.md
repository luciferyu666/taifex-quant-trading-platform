# Partner Profit Sharing

## 1. Purpose

This document separates platform operating revenue, trading capital performance, and strategy marketplace revenue. It is a planning framework only and is not legal, tax, securities, or futures advisory advice.

## 2. Three Revenue Pools

1. Platform operating revenue: SaaS, data services, AI diagnostics, training, implementation, enterprise licensing.
2. Trading capital performance: future self-funded or externally funded trading arrangements, if legally appropriate.
3. Strategy marketplace revenue: strategy distribution and review workflows.

## 3. Role Definitions

- Capital provider: supplies trading capital or working capital.
- Strategy/research contributor: develops or validates strategy research.
- Platform technology/risk contributor: builds infrastructure, Risk Engine, OMS, broker gateway, auditability.
- Trading operations contributor: monitors paper/shadow workflows and future operational procedures.
- Sales/referral contributor: brings qualified leads or enterprise opportunities.
- Review/risk/compliance contributor: performs marketplace review, risk labeling, and compliance workflow.

## 4. Platform Operating Revenue Split

Recommended platform operating net profit allocation:

| Pool | Allocation |
| --- | --- |
| Reinvestment | 50% |
| Shareholder dividend pool | 20% |
| Active partner contribution pool | 20% |
| Risk/legal reserve | 10% |

Net profit should be calculated after infrastructure, data, tax, legal, compliance, payment, support, and operating costs.

## 5. Trading Capital Performance Split

Suggested trading performance pool if self-funded and legally appropriate:

| Role | Allocation |
| --- | --- |
| Capital provider | 75% |
| Strategy/research | 15% |
| Platform technology/risk | 7% |
| Trading operations | 3% |

Trading capital arrangements and performance-based compensation may require legal review. External capital, copy trading, managed accounts, or discretionary trading must not be launched without legal and regulatory review.

## 6. Strategy Marketplace Split

Suggested strategy marketplace split:

| Role | Allocation |
| --- | --- |
| Strategy author | 60% |
| Platform | 30% |
| Review/risk/compliance | 10% |

Marketplace revenue should not be marketed as copy trading or guaranteed profit. Strategy distribution requires risk labels, author terms, review process, and compliance review.

## 7. Sales and Referral Commissions

Early sales partners can receive:
- one-time referral commissions
- milestone bonuses
- revenue-share with time limits
- enterprise pilot success fees

Commission terms should define eligible leads, close date, payment timing, clawback, and conflict restrictions.

## 8. Phantom Equity

Phantom equity can reward early contributors without immediate formal equity transfer. It should define vesting, payout trigger, forfeiture, confidentiality, and IP assignment.

## 9. Formal Equity and Vesting

Formal equity should require:
- vesting
- IP assignment
- confidentiality
- non-solicitation where appropriate
- exit clauses
- measurable contribution
- board or founder approval

## 10. Milestone Bonuses

Possible milestones:
- first paid SaaS cohort
- first enterprise pilot
- first data service customer
- first broker partnership memorandum
- first marketplace contributor cohort
- successful security review package

## 11. Governance Rules

- Separate operating company revenue from trading capital revenue.
- Keep financial records auditable.
- Require written agreements.
- Define decision rights.
- Keep risk/legal reserve funded.
- Do not mix customer trading funds with platform operating accounts.

## 12. Exit and Buyback Principles

Partner arrangements should define:
- departure rights
- unvested forfeiture
- buyback formula
- confidentiality survival
- IP ownership
- customer relationship ownership

## 13. Required Agreements

- Founder or partner agreement.
- Contractor or advisor agreement.
- IP assignment.
- Confidentiality agreement.
- Referral or sales commission agreement.
- Marketplace author terms.
- Enterprise statement of work.

## 14. Example Scenarios

Example 1: Referral partner brings an enterprise pilot. Use a fixed commission or milestone bonus instead of permanent equity.

Example 2: Strategy developer publishes to marketplace. Use 60/30/10 split after review, tax, refund, payment, and platform costs.

Example 3: Internal team uses self-funded capital in a future legally reviewed structure. Use a documented trading performance pool only after legal review.

## 15. Recommended Default Structure

Default recommendation:
- Use SaaS and enterprise revenue as the core business model.
- Use commissions and milestone bonuses for early contributors.
- Use phantom equity before formal equity when contribution scope is uncertain.
- Reserve formal equity for long-term, measurable, vested contributions.
- Keep performance-based models compliance-dependent and inactive until legal review is complete.
